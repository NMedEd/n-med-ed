Etiological Classification Underlying Cause of Venous Disease · Ec · Ep · Es · En
Code Etiology Onset / Mechanism Description Examples
Ec
 CongenitalPRESENT FROM BIRTH Developmental anomaly Condition present since birth, though may not manifest until later; abnormality in venous development during embryogenesis Klippel-Trénaunay
Ep
 PrimaryUNKNOWN CAUSE Idiopathic / intrinsic No identifiable underlying cause; likely genetic predisposition; valve incompetence without previous DVT or trauma Idiopathic Varices
Es
 SecondaryKNOWN CAUSE Acquired / extrinsic Identifiable cause such as post-thrombotic syndrome, trauma, or extrinsic compression; DVT is the most common secondary cause Post-DVT · Trauma
En
 No Cause IdentifiedNON-IDENTIFIABLE Unclassifiable Venous disease present but no etiology can be identified after thorough investigation; documented after workup is complete Unclassified
A
Anatomical Classification Venous System Segment Involved · As (Superficial) · Ap (Perforator) · Ad (Deep) · An (No venous location)
Code Segment System Anatomical Sites Included Key Vessels
As
 Superficial VeinsABOVE DEEP FASCIA Superficial venous system Telangiectasias, reticular veins, great saphenous vein (thigh & leg), small saphenous vein, non-saphenous veins, saphenofemoral & saphenopopliteal junctions GSV · SSV · Junctions
Ap
 Perforator VeinsFASCIAL PENETRATORS Perforating venous system Perforating veins of thigh (Dodd) and calf (Boyd, Cockett / paratibial); connect superficial to deep system; incompetent perforators drive reflux Cockett · Dodd · Boyd
Ad
 Deep VeinsBELOW DEEP FASCIA Deep venous system Inferior vena cava, iliac veins (common, internal, external), pelvic veins, femoral vein, deep femoral vein, popliteal, tibial, peroneal, muscular (gastrocnemial, soleal) veins IVC · Iliofemoral · Pop.
An
 No LocationNOT IDENTIFIABLE Unidentified segment No venous location identified despite complete investigation; used when anatomical cause cannot be determined by duplex ultrasound or other imaging Unidentified
P
Pathophysiological Classification Underlying Haemodynamic Mechanism · Pr (Reflux) · Po (Obstruction) · Pr,o (Both) · Pn (No cause)
Code Mechanism Haemodynamics Pathophysiological Description Investigation
Pr
 RefluxVALVULAR INCOMPETENCE Retrograde blood flow Venous valve incompetence causing retrograde flow; ambulatory venous hypertension; most common mechanism in primary varicose veins; reflux >0.5s (superficial) or >1s (deep) Duplex — Reflux Test
Po
 ObstructionOUTFLOW IMPAIRMENT Reduced venous outflow Partial or complete obstruction of venous outflow; caused by thrombosis (DVT), extrinsic compression (May-Thurner), or intraluminal web; elevated venous pressures Duplex · IVUS · CT
Pr,o
 Reflux + ObstructionCOMBINED MECHANISM Mixed haemodynamics Coexisting reflux and obstruction; most severe haemodynamic combination; common in post-thrombotic syndrome; progressive valve destruction after DVT leads to combined pathology Multi-modal Imaging
Pn
 No MechanismNOT IDENTIFIABLE Haemodynamically silent No pathophysiological mechanism identified; venous disease present without demonstrable reflux or obstruction on duplex assessment; requires documentation after full investigation Documented Workup
